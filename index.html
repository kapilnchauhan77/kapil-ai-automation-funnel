§ 09Frequently asked

Questions before the first call.

What kinds of workflows do you typically automate?

Document-heavy and rules-heavy ones — invoice processing, KYC / KYB review, customs declarations, bookkeeping coding, support-ticket triage, RFP and contract analysis, and internal dashboards that pull from disconnected tools. If a step looks like “read this document → enter these numbers → check this against that,” it’s almost certainly automatable.

How much does an AI Automation Audit cost?

The audit is a fixed-scope, fixed-timeline engagement priced per project. Most audits land between $1.5k–$3.5k depending on operational complexity. The audit fee credits toward any follow-on build engagement. Book a free 20-minute call for a specific quote.

How long does it take from audit to a working system?

The audit ships in 7–10 days as a written PDF plus a walkthrough call. A full build engagement typically ships in 4–8 weeks depending on integrations. No open-ended retainers — every engagement has a fixed scope and a date.

What tools and platforms do you integrate with?

Common business stacks: Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks, Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Slack, Stripe, NetSuite, and most custom ERPs. AI layer: OpenAI, Anthropic, and Gemini APIs, LangChain, LlamaIndex, plus vector databases (Pinecone, Qdrant, Azure AI Search). Cloud: AWS, GCP, Azure. If your stack isn’t listed, ask.

Do you work outside the US, UK, Australia, and Singapore?

Yes — engagements run remotely with teams across Europe, New Zealand, and Asia. Work happens primarily over video calls and async written specs, with a kickoff and handover call per phase.

What about sensitive data — HIPAA, GDPR, financial records?

NDAs are standard. For regulated data the AI workflows deploy inside your cloud (AWS, Azure, or GCP) so no document leaves your perimeter, and only enterprise / business-tier LLM endpoints are used — no training on your data, with data residency available where required.
